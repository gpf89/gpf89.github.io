  • Visualising the SUMO data in the traffic client. Interpolating motion and orientation of the vehicles. Interpolating the vehicle positions along splines connecting them was done by our lead developer for which I provided some mathematical support and then I built on that work to to ensure that the vehicle kinematics were those of a car being driven by its wheels. This is a perfect example of where my physicist past came in handy as the usual treatments of vehicle turning kinematics derive the motion of the body of the vehicle from its wheels as that is where the the motive force comes from. However SUMO uses the front bumper as the vehicle position so vehicle body and corresponding wheel motion had to be determined from this. Solving the problem mathematically and then turning it into code. Ensuring that the code remains performant. Added vehicle types which introduced subtleties about representing the right type of vehicle with a correct type but randomised prefab. Involved adding new messages to the protocol, amending the server, road editor and traffic client to handle vehicle classes and ensure consistency between the vehicle distribution generated by the road editor and represent by the client.
  • collision avoidance. The driving simulator had a problem of traffic vehicles occasionally driving through the driver. This was due to 0.5sec of lag between where the sriver saw ones self with respect to the traffic and where the SUMO simulation thoguht that the driver was. Not being able to further bring that delay time down on account of needing to buffer some vehicle positions for the spline interpolation between them we had to detect when a collision was about to happen and slow the offending traffic vehicle down to avoid the collision. The catch is that then this needs to be communcated back to the simulation via the server to keep them synchronised. One of the ways in which MXT’sSUMO visualisationwas uniquewas that itwas (nearly) real-time and interactive. Itwas vital to not break this for an immersive experience.
