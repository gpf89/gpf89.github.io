I am a physicist having transitioned into Virtual Reality. During this transition I became interested in the capacity of games & VR as a means of learning about the abstract ideas of maths and physics recruiting the intuitive and experiential faculties of the human body to supplement its analytical ones. As a guiding principle I hold that virtual reality, for this purpose, should not aim to provide a facsimile of reality but make available otherwise inaccessible realms of experience in ways in which it is uniquely positioned, amongst the other technologies, to do.

Virtual reality

I morked at MXT on traffic and infrastructure visualisation. My responsibility was making sure that traffic looked and behaved realistically and ran smoothly. Bringing the analytical & numerical faculties and the practice of comparing a contructed model to observations of reality and engaging in its recursive refinement from physics I could find a place in the team where I could bring these skills to bear on refining the traffic in our driving simulator while gaining full-stack experience in its implementation.

Physics

I spent my physics career boradly in the area of optics with a detour into electron optics. My thesis dealt with three fairly disparate problems broadly fitting into the category of which-path problems. After some more study of the quantum properties of light in the Quantum Theory Group at the University of Glasgow I spent some time in classical imaging at in the Optics and Imaging concepts groups and electron miscroscope analysis of semiconductor samples. My programming experience here includes contributions to the relativistic ray-tracer DrTIM.

The rest of life

I try to look at life through as many lenses as I can. My artistic interest include drawing and music. My journey in these has been almost entirely autodidactic and have been greatly instructive in learning how to learn. I occasionally combine art and physics in trying to illustrate physical concepts through an artistic rather than academic medium.
